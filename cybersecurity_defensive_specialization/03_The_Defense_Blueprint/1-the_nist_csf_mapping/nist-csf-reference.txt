================================================================================
          NIST CYBERSECURITY FRAMEWORK (CSF) 2.0
          Reference Summary for MedDefense Health Systems
          Compiled for Project 0x03: The Defense Blueprint
================================================================================

OVERVIEW:
  Publisher: National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST)
  Version: 2.0 (released February 2024)
  Scope: Voluntary framework applicable to all organizations
  Structure: 6 Functions > Categories > Subcategories

========================================================================
FUNCTION 1: GOVERN (GV) -- New in CSF 2.0
========================================================================
Purpose: Establish, communicate, and monitor the organization's
cybersecurity risk management strategy, expectations, and policy.

Key Categories:
  GV.OC: Organizational Context
    - The organization's mission, stakeholder expectations, and
      legal/regulatory requirements are understood
  GV.RM: Risk Management Strategy
    - Risk appetite, tolerance, and strategy are established
  GV.RR: Roles, Responsibilities, and Authorities
    - Cybersecurity roles and authorities are defined
  GV.PO: Policy
    - Cybersecurity policy is established and communicated
  GV.OV: Oversight
    - Results of cybersecurity activities are used to adjust strategy
  GV.SC: Cybersecurity Supply Chain Risk Management
    - Supply chain risks are identified, assessed, and managed

Assessment Questions for MedDefense:
  - Does MedDefense have a documented cybersecurity strategy?
  - Are cybersecurity roles formally assigned?
  - Does a formal security policy exist (AUP, incident response)?
  - Is there a process for reviewing and updating security activities?

========================================================================
FUNCTION 2: IDENTIFY (ID)
========================================================================
Purpose: Understand the organization's current cybersecurity risks.

Key Categories:
  ID.AM: Asset Management
    - Physical and software assets are inventoried
    - Data flows and external information systems are mapped
  ID.RA: Risk Assessment
    - Threats, vulnerabilities, likelihood, and impact are identified
    - Risk responses are determined
  ID.IM: Improvement
    - Improvements are identified from assessments and exercises

Assessment Questions for MedDefense:
  - Is there a comprehensive asset inventory?
  - Are risks formally assessed with likelihood and impact?
  - Has a risk assessment been performed (threat + vulnerability)?
  - Are lessons learned from incidents documented?

========================================================================
FUNCTION 3: PROTECT (PR)
========================================================================
Purpose: Use safeguards to manage cybersecurity risks.

Key Categories:
  PR.AA: Identity Management, Authentication, and Access Control
    - Identities and credentials are managed
    - Access permissions are enforced using least privilege
    - MFA is implemented where appropriate
  PR.AT: Awareness and Training
    - Users are informed and trained
  PR.DS: Data Security
    - Data is protected at rest, in transit, and in use
  PR.PS: Platform Security
    - Hardware, software, and services are managed
    - Secure configuration baselines are maintained
  PR.IR: Technology Infrastructure Resilience
    - Security architectures are managed to protect assets

Assessment Questions for MedDefense:
  - Is MFA deployed on critical systems?
  - Are endpoints hardened with secure baselines?
  - Is data encrypted at rest and in transit?
  - Is the network segmented?
  - Are users trained on security awareness?

========================================================================
FUNCTION 4: DETECT (DE)
========================================================================
Purpose: Find and analyze possible cybersecurity attacks and compromises.

Key Categories:
  DE.CM: Continuous Monitoring
    - Networks and systems are monitored for cybersecurity events
    - Physical environment is monitored
  DE.AE: Adverse Event Analysis
    - Anomalies and potential events are analyzed
    - Events are correlated from multiple sources

Assessment Questions for MedDefense:
  - Is there a SIEM or centralized logging?
  - Are network traffic anomalies detected?
  - Are alerts investigated and correlated?
  - Is there 24/7 monitoring capability?

========================================================================
FUNCTION 5: RESPOND (RS)
========================================================================
Purpose: Take action regarding detected cybersecurity incidents.

Key Categories:
  RS.MA: Incident Management
    - Incidents are triaged, escalated, and resolved
    - Incident response plan is executed
  RS.AN: Incident Analysis
    - Investigation determines scope and impact
    - Forensics are performed
  RS.CO: Incident Reporting and Communication
    - Internal and external stakeholders are notified
  RS.MI: Incident Mitigation
    - Actions are taken to contain and eradicate incidents

Assessment Questions for MedDefense:
  - Does an incident response plan exist?
  - Has the IR plan been tested?
  - Are communication procedures defined for breach notification?
  - Can MedDefense contain an active incident?

========================================================================
FUNCTION 6: RECOVER (RC)
========================================================================
Purpose: Restore assets and operations affected by cybersecurity incidents.

Key Categories:
  RC.RP: Incident Recovery Plan Execution
    - Recovery plan is executed during or after an incident
  RC.CO: Incident Recovery Communication
    - Recovery activities are communicated to stakeholders

Assessment Questions for MedDefense:
  - Are backups tested and recoverable?
  - Is the backup infrastructure isolated from production?
  - Can MedDefense restore operations within defined RTOs?
  - Is there a business continuity plan?
